Who am I?

Hi, I'm Aydan Ligon, a passionate creative and entrepreneur based in Berlin. I'm dedicated to storytelling, digital art, and inspiring others to embrace their creativity. Bilingual in English and German, I strive to make meaningful connections through my potratis and share impactful stories.

My Goals

My primary goals are to excel in the storytime animation community, finish my education and get a stable non-artistic career that blends creativity with meaningful contributions to the world. I also aim to maintain focus, build healthy habits, and inspire others through my entrepreneurial journey.

My Background

Born on March 20, 2005, I grew up bilingual in German and English, with roots in Germany and the U.S.. My main courses in school were art and English, I've now passed my abitur ^^ and I'm trying to make something of myself.

What I'm doing now

I am preparing for a role in tax services while continuing to develop my creative skills and work on personal projects. I've also recently deleted games to focus on productivity and maintain consistent habits.

My Current Priorities

I'm prioritizing staying disciplined in my work, improving my health and fitness, and focusing on storytelling projects that inspire and bring joy to others, trying to make YouTube work for me whilest optimizeing for health and financial liberty, putting in the hours so I can profit from delayed gratitude and have more fun later in life.

My Values

I value family, fitness, and freedom. I strive to live joyfully, engaged, and confidently, guided by my goal to tell inspiring stories that encourage creativity and happiness in others.

Things that fascinate me

Digital art, portraiture, videography, game design, psychology, science, and entrepreneurship captivate me. I love creating, experimenting, and participating in initiatives like Makeathon to learn and innovate.

Biographical Information

I live in Berlin and my creative work has been featured in local restaurants :>, and I enjoy engaging with local street/art communities. I've also visited the U.S. frequently and was inspired by a visit to Harvard during my last trip.
